Understanding the basics

How home care pricing works in Australia

There are two main ways to access home care. Here's a plain-language breakdown of each.

Government-funded

Support at Home Program

The Australian Government's Support at Home program provides funding to eligible older Australians to access care in their own home. The government allocates a quarterly budget based on your assessed classification — there are 8 classification levels.

You use that budget to purchase care hours and services from an approved provider like J.PEER Health. Depending on your financial circumstances, you may have a small income-tested contribution, but many clients pay nothing beyond their government allocation.

  • Requires an ACAT assessment through My Aged Care
  • Government pays the majority of your care costs
  • You choose your provider and your services
  • J.PEER Health guides you through every step
Private / Self-funded

Private Care

Private care means you pay directly for the care you receive. There's no waiting for an assessment, no government allocation — just the support you need, when you need it. Ideal for families who want to begin care immediately.

Private care is also a great option for people awaiting a government assessment, those who want additional hours beyond their funded budget, or short-term needs like post-hospital recovery.

  • No assessment required — care can start quickly
  • Transparent hourly rates, no surprises
  • Flexible hours — as few or as many as you need
  • Seamless transition to funded care when approved
Government funding

Support at Home funding levels

The government classifies care needs into 8 levels and assigns a quarterly budget accordingly. Your assessment determines your level.

Level 1

Entry-level support

~$5,000–$7,000/yr

Light assistance — help around the house and occasional personal care.

Level 2

Low-level support

~$7,000–$12,000/yr

Regular help with daily tasks, personal care, and social support.

Level 3

Low-medium support

~$12,000–$17,000/yr

More frequent assistance with a wider range of daily living activities.

Level 4

Medium support

~$17,000–$22,000/yr

Daily care and support, often including some nursing or health support.

Level 5

Medium-high support

~$22,000–$28,000/yr

Substantial daily support across personal care, domestic, and nursing.

Level 6

High support

~$28,000–$35,000/yr

High-frequency care, complex health needs, and clinical support.

Level 7

Very high support

~$35,000–$45,000/yr

Intensive daily care, often including overnight or specialist nursing.

Level 8

Intensive support

~$45,000–$60,000/yr

The highest level of home-based support for complex, ongoing needs.

Budgets shown are approximate annual equivalents. Exact quarterly budgets are confirmed by My Aged Care following your assessment. Call My Aged Care on 1800 200 422 or visit myagedcare.gov.au to get started. J.PEER Health can help you through the process — just give us a call.
Our fee structure

J.PEER Health pricing — exactly what you'll pay

Care management fee

A competitive percentage of your quarterly Support at Home budget. This covers all care coordination, scheduling, care plan management, quality reviews, and after-hours support. Our management fees are among the most transparent in Melbourne's south-east.

We'll explain the exact percentage when we meet — call us or submit an enquiry to get the conversation started.

Hourly care rates

Hourly rates vary by service type, time of day, and whether care is required on weekends or public holidays — in line with industry standards. We'll always show you the exact hourly rate for each service before you agree to anything.

Three types of overnight care — choose what fits

Not all overnight needs are the same. We offer three distinct care options, from full-time live-in care to targeted nighttime support.

Live-in Care

A dedicated carer lives in your home, providing continuous support day and night. Ideal for clients with high needs, dementia, or those who simply need constant reassurance that someone is always present.

Best for: High-need clients, dementia, post-hospital recovery

Sleepover Care

A carer sleeps on-site and responds immediately when needed. The carer rests but is available the moment something happens. Provides peace of mind without the cost of active overnight care.

Best for: Fall risk, mild dementia, family carer relief

Active Overnight Care

A carer remains awake and active throughout the night. For clients who wake frequently, require regular repositioning, or have complex clinical needs that don't pause after midnight.

Best for: Frequent night waking, complex care, palliative support

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between live-in and overnight care?

Live-in care means a carer stays in your home full-time, supporting both day and night needs. Overnight care is specifically for nighttime hours — the carer either sleeps on-site and responds when needed (sleepover care) or remains awake throughout the night (active overnight care). We'll discuss your specific situation and recommend the right option.

Is someone always awake during overnight care?

It depends on the care type. Active overnight care involves a carer who remains awake throughout. Sleepover care involves a carer who sleeps on-site but responds immediately when needed. For clients with dementia, frequent night waking, or high fall risk, active overnight care is usually recommended.

Can I have overnight care just a few nights a week?

Yes. Overnight care doesn't have to be every night. Some families arrange overnight support on specific nights — for example, when family carers need to rest. We'll build a schedule that works for your household.

Is 24/7 care covered by Support at Home?

Overnight and 24/7 care can be funded through your Support at Home budget, depending on your classification level. Higher classification levels have larger budgets that can accommodate overnight support. Private overnight care is also available. Call us to discuss what's right for your situation.

How does overnight care pricing work?

Overnight care is priced differently from daytime care — it depends on whether sleepover or active overnight care is required. We provide a clear, written fee schedule before care begins. Call us and we'll explain the options honestly.

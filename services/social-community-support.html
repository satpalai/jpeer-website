What's included in social & community support

Connection is a care need, not a luxury. Our social support services are designed to keep your loved one engaged, active, and genuinely part of life.

Companionship visits

Genuine conversation, shared activities, and unhurried time together — not a quick welfare check, but real human connection.

Accompanied outings

Shopping trips, park walks, café visits, medical appointments — your carer accompanies and supports throughout.

Cultural & religious events

Temple visits, cultural celebrations, community gatherings — we support full participation in the traditions that matter.

Technology assistance

Help with video calls to family, using smartphones, tablets, or computers — staying connected to the people who matter most.

Social group participation

Support attending community groups, day programs, exercise classes, or social clubs — keeping your loved one active in their community.

Shopping & errands

Grocery runs, pharmacy trips, post office visits — your carer handles the logistics so your loved one maintains their independence.

Frequently asked questions

What counts as social support under Support at Home?

Social and community support includes accompanied outings, transport to community events, shopping trips, companionship visits at home, assistance with technology and video calls, and support attending cultural or religious events. If an activity helps your loved one stay connected and engaged with life, it likely qualifies.

Can my carer take me to a cultural or religious event?

Absolutely. We consider attendance at cultural celebrations, religious services, and community events to be an essential part of wellbeing, not an optional extra. Our carers are trained to support participation in events that matter to you — respectfully and enthusiastically.

How is social support different from personal care?

Personal care focuses on physical tasks — bathing, dressing, grooming. Social support focuses on connection, community access, and engagement. Many of our clients receive both as part of a combined care plan that addresses all aspects of daily life.

Can I get both social support and personal care?

Yes. Most of our clients combine services. We'll design a care plan that covers everything you need — personal care in the mornings, social support for outings, domestic assistance weekly. Your plan is built around your life, not a menu of standard packages.

How do I know the carer will actually connect with my parent?

We match carers based on language, personality, cultural background, and shared interests. It's not a random assignment — it's a thoughtful introduction. If the connection isn't right, we'll find a better match. Genuine relationship is the whole point.
